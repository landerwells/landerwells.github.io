Ideas are not software; they are another layer of abstraction higher. If the
brain is hardware, the software is the signals being sent and received by the
neurons inside. Ideas are what forms on top of theses signals, how we perceive
them, and how we give them meaning.

> Thus, you are not comparing hardware, you are not comparing software---you are
> comparing "etherware"---the pure concepts which lie back of the software. There
> is some sort of abstract "conceptual skeleton" which must be lifted out of low
> levels before you can carry out a meaningful comparison of two programs in
> different computer languages, of two animals, or of two sentences in different
> natural languages.
