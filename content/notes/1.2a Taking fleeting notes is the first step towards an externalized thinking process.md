Taking fleeting notes is the first step towards an externalized thinking
process. By allowing your brain to offload thoughts as you have them, it clears
the way for more to take their place. You won't forget important ideas if you
write them down in a place that you are sure to come back to.

> Both fleeting and reference notes are fundamental to the system presented in
> this book as they represent the very beginnings of externalized thinking,
> allowing you to quickly capture thoughts and ideas so you can come back to them
> later.
