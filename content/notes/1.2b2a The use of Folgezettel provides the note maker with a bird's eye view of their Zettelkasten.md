The use of Folgezettel provides the note maker with a bird's eye view of their
Zettelkasten. Traditionally, files are sorted in alphabetical order, which while
nice when trying to find a certain word in a dictionary, doesn't help much when
trying to locate a particular note. Using Folgezettel leverages this sorting to
disply notes related to each other close together. Thoughts can be displayed
through alphanumeric chains, and the longer a chain is, the more developed the
thought.


## see [Titles in a Zettelkasten should be declarative]({{< relref "1.6 Titles in a Zettelkasten should be declarative.md" >}}) {#see-titles-in-a-zettelkasten-should-be-declarative--1-dot-6-titles-in-a-zettelkasten-should-be-declarative-dot-md}

Descriptive titles are important to a Zettelkasten as they allow the user to
easily distinguish the contents of a note. Folgezettel takes this a step further
by allowing the note's title to contain information regarding its connections
within the Zettelkasten.
