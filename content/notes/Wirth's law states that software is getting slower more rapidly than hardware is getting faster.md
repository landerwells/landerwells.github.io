In his article "A Plea for Lean Software", Niklaus Wirth was the first to notice
and formally state that the speed and efficiency is slowing faster than hardware
is improving. This has been coined Wirth's Law.

According to Moore's Law, the number of transistors doubles nearly every two
years. This leads to increased efficiency and performance. When these two laws
interact, software essentially stays at the same performance.
