About Me
Who Am I?

My name is Lander Wells! I am currently a junior at Northern Michigan University (NMU) and I am pursuing a Computer Science degree with a minor in Electronics. I started making this website because I enjoyed the process of coding and learning how to use different programming languages.

Outside of school I am a big music nerd. I have been involved with music since preschool when I started taking piano lessons. I learned the fundamentals of music through piano but I was never truly invested in the instrument. I continued taking lessons for a few more years until I began playing the clarinet in 5th grade. I much preferred the clarinet to the piano but my dream was always to play the saxophone. The clarinet was my main instrument through middle school but I finally got the chance to play the saxophone after joining the middle school jazz band. By the time I reached high school I switched to playing the alto sax full time.

In 10th grade, my band teacher told me to audition for the high school jazz band. He was very interested in me playing the baritone/bari sax since there was no one else who could. I hadn’t ever played it before either but I gave it a shot. Jazz band was some of the most fun I had in high school. I played in the top jazz band at both of my high schools for the next three years. I played in numerous concerts and many venues, I have listened to hundreds of hours of jazz music and still haven’t heard enough.

After high school I took a break from music since I was getting a little burnt out. This break turned into an extended leave and it took almost a year for me to get back into playing again, but I did not return to the saxophone. As you can imagine, playing a saxophone in a college dorm wouldn’t turn out too well, and I had been looking into learning how to play an electric bass a lot over the previous year. I made a purchase that I have not regretted at all and bought the bass pictured above, and have continued my musical journey with yet another instrument.
